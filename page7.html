What is the UNHRC?

The United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) is like the global watchdog for human rights. It keeps an eye on countries to make sure they’re treating people fairly, with dignity and respect.

In this committee, countries come together to talk about serious issues like:

  • Freedom of speech
  • Refugee protection
  • Racism and discrimination
  • Unfair treatment by governments

The UNHRC doesn’t just talk—it investigates problems, finds solutions, and helps people whose rights are being ignored.

What do YOU do in MUN as a UNHRC delegate?

You step into the shoes of a country and become a human rights advocate.

Your job?

  • Speak up for people who can’t
  • Work with other countries to find real solutions
  • Learn to debate with empathy, ethics, and logic
  • Build resolutions that can actually bring change

It’s not just about arguing. It’s about making the world a better place—one speech at a time.
